    About Haipo Bakery

    A Kitchen Legacy in the Alleyways

    The story of Haipo Bakery begins in a small alleyway in Changhua County during the 1960s. Founder Master Ah-Bo, with his passion for traditional pastries and exceptional culinary skills, started making cakes and pastries in his home kitchen. Initially specializing in wedding, funeral, and temple festival cakes, Master Ah-Bo gained a reputation in the neighborhood for using quality ingredients at reasonable prices. Locals would say: "For authentic pastries, go find Master Ah-Bo in the alley!"

    Inspiration from the Kitchen God

    As times changed, Master Ah-Bo sought to give traditional pastries new life. One Mid-Autumn Festival Eve, while praying to the Kitchen God for inspiration before preparing his baked goods, he stared at the warm fire and golden salted egg yolks before him. A thought struck him: "What if I blend the fragrance of Western cream with the layers of Chinese shortbread, and fill it with this plump salted egg yolk?" After countless attempts, he successfully created two iconic products that would delight taste buds: "Gu Zao Creamy Shortbread" and "Golden Egg Yolk Pastry."

    Gu Zao Creamy Shortbread

    Made with natural fermented butter instead of traditional lard. The pastry has up to a dozen delicate layers, thin as cicada wings, melting in your mouth to release pure creamy milk aroma and roasted flour fragrance. The filling is a carefully simmered maltose syrup mixed with fine mung bean paste, sweet but not cloying, smooth and mellow.

    Golden Egg Yolk Pastry

    Selecting premium red soil-cured salted duck eggs, first roasted with rice wine to remove fishy taste, then wrapped in specially made black bean paste or smooth taro paste. The crust maintains the same emphasis on multi-layer flakiness, golden and alluring. The perfect balance of sweet and salty creates a rich flavor profile, making it a top choice for Mid-Autumn gifts.

    These two star products quickly captured hearts with their exquisite texture and quality ingredients. The name "Haibo Bakery" spread across Taiwan's pastry industry through these "one crispy, one cake" signature items.

    Hidden Gem, Beloved by All

    Though Haipo Bakery's main store hides in a small alley beside a temple in a quiet town, without flashy signs, it has built its reputation through word-of-mouth. Loyal customers follow the aroma of fresh butter and baked pastries, or rely on the "food map" passed down by regulars to find this hidden gem. During peak seasons like Mid-Autumn Festival or when new batches emerge, long queues often form outside the store. The owner always apologizes to waiting customers with a smile: "So many people coming, so many people coming. Good things are worth waiting for!" This sincerity and apology only confirm the old saying: "A shop need not be large; with good taste, it will be known."

    Staying True to Tradition, Embracing Modernity

    From early wedding and funeral cakes to the more refined pastries demanded in the 1960s that gave birth to "Gu Zao Creamy Shortbread" and "Golden Egg Yolk Pastry," Haipo Bakery has evolved with the times. While adapting to modern preferences, we remain committed to our core values. Whether for daily tea snacks, festive gifts, or Mid-Autumn reunions, our elegant packaging and consistent quality always convey the most sincere wishes.

    Our Promise Never Changes

    Over the years, Haipo Bakery has upheld the philosophy of "integrity, quality, and gratitude." We believe the secret to delicious pastries lies not in fancy packaging, but in precise oven temperature control, uncompromising use of premium ingredients, and the heartfelt warmth passed through every step of traditional craftsmanship.

    Haipo Bakery - Using a century of handcrafting expertise to bake the most delightful Taiwanese pastry flavors. Welcome to visit our alleyway store, follow the aroma of butter and baked pastries, and taste this time-honored delicacy that carries our heartfelt wishes. Let us package this sincere blessing into your gift box to share happiness with loved ones.
